Before Your Session Checklist



  1. Pick Out Your Clothes Early

    We highly reccommend working with your photographer to get suggestions on what to wear for your sessions. Themes and clothing can make a big difference in elevating an images’ overall look. Once you know what you should wear, make sure to assemble your wardrobe a few days before your session. Get items laundered, ironed, and anything else needed so that you’re not rushing at the last minute. Another helpful tip is to figure out how you’ll change your clothes on location. Check out our Portfolio to get some great ideas.

  2. Gather Your Props

    If you are utilizing any special props for your portraits, make sure to assemble them a few days before the shoot. These props may include anything from a baseball glove, hockey skates, photo of a loved one, or a favorite toy just to name a few.


  3. Bring The Necessities

    Be sure to bring some snacks along. Snack breaks during a session can often make a difference between a happy child and session full of tears. It’s also helpful to plan for clean snacks so that mouths, hands and clothes aren’t stained or messy for pictures. We highly recommend bringing some comfort items to keep your mind and body happy during your shoot.


  4. Get On The Same Page As Your Photographer

    At MEC Photography, our main objective is for you to have a successful photoshoot. It’s a great idea to share photo concepts with your photographer to ensure you are going for the same result. Create a list of the kinds of shots you would like, it helps us plan our time together so you get the most out of your photo session. We can disucss it in our introduction meeting where we get to know each other a littler better. Just so you know, we love sharing Pinterest boards with our clients, feel free to pin away.


  5. Locations

    Simply put, location matters. It helps bring context to your photos. Studio or on location? A park or the mountains? We are here to capture all of your real and authentic moments and everything else in-between. Check out this guide on Newfoundland photography locations.
